Jason Momoa takes no prisoners in this intense action-thriller. When Joe (Momoa) and his father (Stephen Lang) arrive at their remote hunting cabin, they're hoping for a quiet weekend. What they find is a stash of heroin, hidden in the cabin by drug traffickers. When the criminals suddenly descend upon the cabin, Joe and his father must make a kill-or-be-killed stand for survival.

  • Liam H
    A very good film. The marketing of this movie would make you automatically think....a typical B rated action movie. This film is actually very well written and very well acted. The characters are well developed characters, the story is well written and unusually developed for a B rated action flick. The action scenes are very well choreographed and performed. The sound effects are realistic and satisfying. The story is developed and believable. The film deserves extra kudos for showcasing very realistic fighting sequences. The fight scenes are very much punch for punch, gritty scenes. Often the fight scenes we are used to in movies do not have that realistic back and forth as seen here. Also this film desevres credit for tastefully handled gore. There were gorey scenes but just the right amount not too much. Where I would mark this film down is that I wanted more of it to be honest. Some action I feel was substituted so as to develop the story more and the characters. It is a victim of it's own success in this way because I wanted more.

    Excellent setting, great acting, great cast, but terribly scripted and poorly executed action sequences. What could have been an amazing movie is hampered by action sequences that make no sense no matter how hard you try to wrap your brain around them. There is little respect for firearms, basic combat logistics, and the film is loaded with classic chase sequence faux pas. The action sequences feel like they were shot and directed by first year film students. To avoid spoilers I'd compare this to Star Wars The Last Jedi. In The Last Jedi we must be constantly reminded that the rebels are low on light speed gas and the ships all have the same speed limit in sub-light travel so that we can have a prolonged impending doom space scene. This film has to explain the action sequence, because they are so contrived. If you forgive the action direction the rest of this film is pretty gosh darn good!
